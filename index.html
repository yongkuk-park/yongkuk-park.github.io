I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the Artzi Lab at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Harvard Medical School, where my research focuses on engineering nanocarrier-based platforms for cancer immunotherapy and tissue regeneration. I received my Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering with a specialization in bone tissue engineering, during which I developed advanced in vitro bone tissue models to recapitulate the complexity of human bone physiology. My current work integrates biomaterial engineering, nanomedicine, and tumor immunology, with a particular focus on leveraging innate immune activation strategies to improve therapeutic outcomes. I am passionate about bridging fundamental biology with translational engineering to develop next-generation therapeutic platforms.

🔬 Research Interest

  • Immunotherapy
  • Tissue engineering - In vitro tissue model
  • Mechanotransduction, Mechanobiology - Device fabrication for mechanical cell culture
  • Bone regeneration, regenerative medicine
  • Nucleic acid-based drug delivery
